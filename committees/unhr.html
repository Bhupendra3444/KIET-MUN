United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC)

Agenda:- Deliberation on the prevention of discrimination based on religion with special emphasis on islamophobia.

• President: Dr. Shikhar Tripathi
• Vice President: Ms. Adisree Telem


In the Model United Nations conference representing the United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC), there are 65 portfolios allocated to member states. This simulation aims to mirror the structure and functions of the real UNHRC, albeit in a simulated environment where students take on the roles of diplomats representing different countries. The Model UNHRC, like its real-life counterpart, convenes to discuss and address various human rights issues on a global scale. Delegates, representing the 65 member states, engage in debates, negotiations, and the drafting of resolutions aimed at promoting and protecting human rights worldwide.

During the conference, delegates collaborate with the UN Special Procedures, which include special rapporteurs, special representatives, independent experts, and working groups. These mechanisms are crucial in monitoring, investigating, advising, and publicly reporting on specific human rights concerns or situations. Delegates are expected to adhere to the highest standards of diplomacy and advocacy, advocating for the interests and priorities of the countries they represent while also working towards consensus and collective action to address human rights challenges. Ultimately, the Model UNHRC serves as an educational platform for students to enhance their understanding of global issues, diplomacy, negotiation, and consensus-building, all within the context of human rights promotion and protection.
